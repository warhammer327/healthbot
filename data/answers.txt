What is the first-line medication for newly diagnosed Type 2 diabetes?
Metformin, along with comprehensive lifestyle modifications (diet and exercise).

What are the classic symptoms of a myocardial infarction (heart attack) in men?
Chest pain or pressure, pain radiating to the left arm or jaw, shortness of breath, and diaphoresis (sweating).

What is the recommended daily dosage of Aspirin for acute stroke prevention?
For immediate treatment after an ischemic stroke, the typical dose is 160 mg to 325 mg daily, started within 24-48 hours.

Which vaccine is routinely recommended for adults aged 65 and older?
The high-dose or adjuvanted influenza vaccine, the pneumococcal vaccine (PCV20 or PCV15 followed by PPSV23), and the recombinant zoster (shingles) vaccine (RZV).

What is the normal range for an adult's resting heart rate?
Typically between 60 and 100 beats per minute.

What is the primary cause of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)?
Cigarette smoking is the leading cause in developed countries.

What is the initial intervention for anaphylaxis?
Epinephrine (adrenaline), administered via intramuscular injection (auto-injector) into the anterolateral thigh.

How is hypertension (high blood pressure) staged according to the ACC/AHA guidelines?
Elevated (120-129/<80), Stage 1 (130-139/80-89), and Stage 2 (140/90 or higher).

What is the most common type of skin cancer?
Basal cell carcinoma.

What is the first-line treatment for acute migraine attacks?
Over-the-counter NSAIDs (like ibuprofen or naproxen sodium) or triptans (like sumatriptan) for moderate to severe cases.

What is the target HbA1c level for many non-pregnant adults with diabetes?
Less than 7%.

What is the "golden hour" in trauma care?
The critical one-hour period after a traumatic injury, during which prompt medical treatment has the highest probability of preventing death.

Which bacteria is the most common cause of uncomplicated urinary tract infections (UTIs)?
Escherichia coli (E. coli).

What is the purpose of a statin medication?
To lower LDL ("bad") cholesterol levels and reduce the risk of cardiovascular events like heart attack and stroke.

What does a "pulse oximeter" measure?
The oxygen saturation level (SpO2) in the blood, indicating how well oxygen is being delivered to the body.

What is the definition of a fever in an adult?
A body temperature of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher.

What is the most important modifiable risk factor for dementia?
Hypertension (high blood pressure) in midlife is considered a leading modifiable risk factor.

What is the name of the procedure used to open a blocked coronary artery?
Percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI), often referred to as angioplasty with stenting.

What is the standard treatment for bacterial pneumonia?
Antibiotics, the specific type depends on the patient's health history and the suspected bacteria.

What does the Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) assess?
A patient's level of consciousness after a brain injury by evaluating eye, verbal, and motor responses.
